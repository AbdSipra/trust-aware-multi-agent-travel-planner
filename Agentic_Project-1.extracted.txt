Agentic AI – Final Semester Project
FAST National University of Computer & Emerging Sciences
Deadline: 24th Novermber, 2025
1. Project Overview
This semester, you are required to identify a real and practical problem that genuinely
requires an Agentic AI solution. You will design, implement, and evaluate a complete
autonomous system that solves the selected problem.
You must submit two compulsory deliverables:
•A fully functionalAgentic AI system(complete code submission)
•A completeIEEE conference-style research paper
Using Generative AI tools to write or generate any part of your research
paper is strictly prohibited and will result in a zero.You may only use LLMs
inside the Agentic AI system itself (as part of your code), not for the writing of the paper.
2. Complex Computing Requirements
Your project must satisfy NCEAC’s definition of a Complex Computing Problem. You
must ensure that your work demonstrates:
2.1 Depth of Knowledge and Abstract Thinking
You must investigate an open-ended, real-world problem requiring knowledge of multi-
agent systems, LLM reasoning, autonomous planning, tool-use agents, and orchestration
frameworks.
2.2 Multiple Possible Solutions
Your problem must not have a single correct solution. You must justify your design
choices.
2.3 Complex System Development
Your system must involve:
•autonomous agent(s),
•decision-making under uncertainty,
•tool usage and/or API integration,
•communication or coordination (if multi-agent),
•dynamic or unpredictable operation.

4.2 IEEE-Style Research Paper
You must write a 6–8 page paper following IEEE conference formatting. Your paper must
include:
1. Title and Abstract
2. Introduction
3. Problem Identification and Motivation
4. Related Work
5. Methodology and System Architecture
6. Dataset, Tools, or APIs Used
7. Experimental Setup and Metrics
8. Results and Discussion
9. Ethical Considerations and Limitations
10. Conclusion and Future Work

6.1 FCFS Grading Advantage
Presentation grading follows a First-Come-First-Serve (FCFS) policy:
•If the presentation is worth 10 marks, the first group to present will be graded out
of 12.
•The second group will be graded out of 11.
•All remaining groups will be graded out of 10.
This encourages early readiness, discipline, and confidence.
6.2 Research Poster Presentation
You must prepare and present asingle research-style poster. You may design it using
PowerPoint, Canva, Figma, Illustrator, or LaTeX.
Your poster must include:
•Title, authors, affiliation
•Abstract or summary
•Problem statement and motivation
•Why your problem requires an Agentic AI solution
•System architecture diagram
•Methodology and workflow
•Experiments and evaluation metrics
•Visual results and findings
•Limitations, ethics, and societal considerations
•Conclusion and future improvements
7. Academic Integrity
•You can not use AI tools to generate text for your report.
•All content must be fully original.
•Violations will result in a zero for the entire project.
8. Selecting a Strong Problem
You must ensure that your selected problem demonstrates:
•real-world significance,
•clear necessity for Agentic AI,
